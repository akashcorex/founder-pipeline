# Content Engine

Build platform-native content without flattening a real builder's voice into generic creator slop.

## When to Activate

- Writing LinkedIn posts or X threads for Akash's personal brand
- Drafting carousel content, infographic copy, or visual captions
- Repurposing a build log, project, learning, or observation into public content
- Building a content sequence around a product launch, learning arc, or personal milestone

## Non-Negotiables

1. Start from real experience — something I actually built, broke, shipped, or learned.
2. First person. "I built this." "I was wrong about this." "I spent two days debugging this."
3. One post = one real claim, insight, or artifact.
4. Specificity beats everything. Real numbers. Real code paths. Real decisions.
5. No engagement bait. No fake questions. No LinkedIn tropes.

## Voice Profile — Akash

| Dimension | Rule |
|-----------|------|
| **Person** | First person always |
| **Tone** | Sharp, honest, builder-to-builder. Like texting another dev. |
| **Authority** | Earned — show the work, don't claim expertise |
| **Vulnerability** | Share mistakes and uncertainty. That's the story. |
| **Humor** | Dry, understated. Never performative. |
| **Length** | As long as the idea needs. No padding. |

## Source-First Workflow

Before drafting, identify what ACTUALLY happened:
- What did I build? (specific repo, feature, script, agent)
- What went wrong? (specific bug, misassumption, edge case)
- What did I learn? (the insight, not the lesson)
- What's the artifact? (screenshot, demo, number, link)

If the source material is thin, don't pad it — pick a different topic.

## Hard Bans

Delete and rewrite any of these:
- "In today's rapidly evolving landscape"
- "game-changer", "revolutionary", "cutting-edge", "mind-blowing"
- "I'm excited to share" / "I'm thrilled to announce"
- "Here's what I learned" — just say what you learned
- "What do you think?" / "Agree?" / "Thoughts?"
- LinkedIn-style praise stacking ("Shoutout to X for inspiring...")
- Fake vulnerability preamble ("I was nervous to post this...")
- "The best part?" / "Here's the kicker" / "And it gets better"
- Journey / destination metaphors
- Forced casualness ("ngl", "tbh", "lowkey" — unless actually natural)
- Generic "keep building" / "stay hungry" sign-offs

## Platform Adaptation Rules

### LinkedIn
- First person, builder voice
- Expand only enough for context outside the niche
- No fake founder-reflection format
- No closing question just because it's LinkedIn
- The insight is the close. Not a question.

### X
- Open with the sharpest claim or artifact
- Compressed. Every word fights for its place.
- Thread only when a single post collapses the argument
- No hashtags. No "🧵". No engagement padding.
- End on the insight, not a question.

## Quality Gate

Before delivering:
- Every draft sounds like me — not a LinkedIn creator, not a tech influencer
- Every draft contains a real claim, artifact, or insight from actual experience
- No banned vocabulary or patterns remain
- No fake engagement bait
- The close is earned, not tacked on
