# Carousel Format Library — Gen Z Dark Aesthetic

Six carousel formats. Every format uses dark backgrounds, bold typography, and clean visual hierarchy. No corporate templates. No stock-photo aesthetic. No warm cream color schemes.

## Format Selection Rules

```
1. Does the topic feature a shipped artifact, product, or demo with real visuals?
   YES → BUILD_LOG
   NO  → continue

2. Is the topic a numbered framework, principles, or structured breakdown?
   YES → SYSTEM_BREAKDOWN
   NO  → continue

3. Is the topic primarily data, stats, or numbers?
   YES → DATA_STORY
   NO  → continue

4. Is the topic a contrarian take, unpopular opinion, or corrected belief?
   YES → CONTRARIAN_TAKE
   NO  → continue

5. Is the topic a step-by-step method, process, or framework?
   YES → FRAMEWORK
   NO  → continue

6. Default → DARK_MINIMAL (bold statement, clean structure, visual-first)
```

## Shared Design System — Gen Z Dark Aesthetic

### Color Palette

Dark backgrounds:
- Primary bg: `#0a0a0a` (deep black)
- Card bg: `#111111` or `#141414`
- Raised surface: `#1a1a1a`
- Subtle border: `#2a2a2a`

Accent colors (pick one per carousel, use consistently):
- **Purple**: `#7C3AED` — systems, architecture, AI
- **Mint**: `#06D6A0` — growth, SaaS, metrics
- **Coral**: `#FF6B6B` — opinions, takes, stories
- **Amber**: `#F59E0B` — data, numbers, rankings

Text colors:
- Primary: `#FFFFFF` or `#F5F5F5`
- Secondary: `#A1A1A1` or `#888888`
- Muted: `#666666`

### Typography

- **Headlines**: Inter 700 or 800, tracking: -0.5px, sentence case
- **Body**: Inter 400 or 500, 18-22px, line-height: 1.5
- **Numbers**: Inter 800, oversized (48-72px for hero stats)
- **Accents**: Instrument Serif italic for pull quotes or emphasis (optional)
- No Instrument Serif for body — only for one-emphasis moments

System font stack fallback: `Inter, -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, "Segoe UI", sans-serif`

### Layout Rules

- 1080×1080px square format
- Generous padding: 60-80px on all sides
- One clear focal point per slide
- No drop shadows
- No gradients (use solid backgrounds, subtle borders instead)
- No emoji as design elements (text emoji ok if earned)
- Slide number in top-right corner, small, subtle (#444 or #555)

### Visual Treatment

- Photography: dark, moody, high contrast. Unsplash search with keywords like "technology dark", "code dark", "server", "minimal dark"
- Use full-bleed images with dark overlay (rgba(0,0,0,0.5)) for slides with photo backgrounds
- Max one image per slide
- Slides without images: bold typography + geometric shapes or subtle patterns
- No clipart. No illustrations. No corporate icons.

## FORMAT 1: BUILD_LOG

**When:** Shipped artifact, product, demo, or project with real visuals.

**Structure:**
- Slide 1: Project name + one-line hook (big bold text)
- Slide 2: The problem I was solving (with context image)
- Slide 3-4: Key features or architecture (screenshots as full-bleed backgrounds)
- Slide 5: What I learned / what broke
- Slide 6: Numbers or outcome (hero stat)
- Slide 7: CTA — "Building in public. Follow for more."

**Colors:** Purple accent (#7C3AED) for tech/AI builds. Mint (#06D6A0) for SaaS/products.

---

## FORMAT 2: SYSTEM_BREAKDOWN

**When:** Numbered framework, principles, or structured breakdown of a concept.

**Structure:**
- Slide 1: Bold title — "How I [verb] [concept]" or "[N] principles for [outcome]"
- Slide 2: Context — why this matters, what I used to do wrong
- Slide 3-5: Three core principles (one per slide, large number + 1-2 sentences)
- Slide 6: The insight — what ties it all together
- Slide 7: CTA — "Steal this framework. Follow for more."

**Colors:** Purple for systems/architecture. Mint for practical frameworks.

**Typography:** Numbers at 72px Bold (800). Labels at 28px.

---

## FORMAT 3: DATA_STORY

**When:** Stats-driven, numbers-first, data narrative.

**Structure:**
- Slide 1: The surprising stat — hero number (72px) + context
- Slide 2: Why this number is surprising (baseline / expectation)
- Slide 3-5: Supporting data points (clean bar or number grid)
- Slide 6: What this means — the implication for builders
- Slide 7: CTA — "Data-backed insights. Follow for more."

**Colors:** Amber accent (#F59E0B). Numbers in white on dark bg.

---

## FORMAT 4: CONTRARIAN_TAKE

**When:** Unpopular opinion, corrected belief, myth-busting.

**Structure:**
- Slide 1: "I was wrong about [belief]" or "Most devs [common belief]. Here's why they're wrong."
- Slide 2: The common belief — what everyone says
- Slide 3: My experience that contradicted it
- Slide 4: The evidence — what actually happened
- Slide 5: The corrected belief
- Slide 6: What I do now instead
- Slide 7: CTA — "Questioning defaults. Follow for more."

**Colors:** Coral accent (#FF6B6B). Bold, punchy.

---

## FORMAT 5: FRAMEWORK

**When:** Step-by-step method, process, or mental model.

**Structure:**
- Slide 1: Framework name + one-line promise
- Slide 2: Step 1 — "Start with [X]" (large step number)
- Slide 3: Step 2 — "Then [Y]"
- Slide 4: Step 3 — "Finally [Z]"
- Slide 5: Real example — how I applied this to something specific
- Slide 6: The result — what changed
- Slide 7: CTA — "Save this framework. Follow for more."

**Colors:** Mint accent (#06D6A0) for growth/process. Purple for technical frameworks.

---

## FORMAT 6: DARK_MINIMAL

**When:** Bold statement, visual-first, clean structure. Default when no other format fits.

**Structure:**
- Slide 1: Bold statement — 6-8 words, massive text, no image
- Slide 2: The problem — one image (full bleed, dark overlay) + 1 sentence
- Slide 3-5: Three supporting points — clean layout, one visual element each
- Slide 6: The insight — bold text, minimal
- Slide 7: CTA — clean, single line, no clutter

**Colors:** Purple or mint, depending on topic. Let the typography do the work.
