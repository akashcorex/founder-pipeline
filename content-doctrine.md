# Content Doctrine — Akash Laha

## Brand Positioning

Akash is a builder documenting his transformation from a developer into a technical founder, sharing every lesson, experiment, win, and setback along the way while relentlessly pursuing excellence in technology, business, and life.

**The single perception:** Akash is an ambitious full-stack developer working inside a startup while building his own ventures, publicly documenting his journey toward becoming a world-class technical founder.

## Core Identity

- Full-stack developer working at a startup
- Simultaneously building own ventures and products
- Every post = one chapter in a long-term story
- Journey: ordinary developer → stronger engineer → better builder → capable entrepreneur → wealth, impact, recognition through technology
- Nothing to sell yet — everything to build and share

## Content Lanes (what we write about)

| Lane | Angle |
|------|-------|
| **Building in public** | What I'm building right now, why, what I'm learning |
| **System design & architecture** | How I think about backend design, tradeoffs I face |
| **AI agents & automation** | Experiments with AI agents, workflows I automate |
| **SaaS & startup execution** | Lessons from building products, launching, iterating |
| **Business strategy** | How I think about markets, distribution, monetization |
| **Engineering mastery** | Deep dives into patterns, principles, hard problems |
| **Founder psychology** | The mental game, setbacks, resilience, growth |
| **Technical founder journey** | The daily reality — code, decisions, progress |

## Topic Filter (Reach, Stakes, Altitude, Edge)

Every post must pass all four:
- **Reach** — Would another 22-35 year old developer/builder care? Is it broad enough?
- **Stakes** — Is there a cost to getting this wrong? A reward for getting it right?
- **Altitude** — Is this about systems, patterns, strategy? Not just syntax or config.
- **Edge** — Does this contain a real insight from my actual experience? Not recycled takes.

## DROP List (banned topics)

- Generic coding tutorials or "how to learn X in Y days"
- Tool configuration walkthroughs (set up ESLint, configure Docker)
- "Steal this prompt" or copy-paste AI workflow posts
- SaaS metrics porn (ARR graphs, MRR screenshots without insight)
- Indie-hacker build-in-public cliches (stripe screenshots, "I made $X")
- Dry news relay without original analysis
- "Hot takes" that are just popular opinions restated
- Comparison content (React vs Vue, Python vs Go — unless you built with both)
- LinkedIn engagement bait, "Agree?" posts, fake polls
- Personal productivity porn (my 5AM routine, my Notion setup)

## AMPLIFY List (what to lean into)

- "Here's what I built" — shipped artifacts, products, demos
- "Here's what broke" — debugging stories, incident postmortems
- "Here's the tradeoff" — architecture decisions with real reasoning
- "Here's what I was wrong about" — corrected beliefs from real experience
- "Here's the system" — mental models, frameworks, ways of thinking
- "Here's the insight" — pattern recognition from building and shipping
- "Here's the numbers" — real data from real products

## Voice

| Rule | Guide |
|------|-------|
| **Person** | First person ("I built", "I learned", "I was wrong") |
| **Tone** | Sharp, honest, slightly informal — like talking to another builder |
| **Authority** | Earned through specificity, not claims |
| **Vulnerability** | Share mistakes and uncertainty — it's part of the journey |
| **No hype** | Never "game-changer", "revolutionary", "mind-blowing" |
| **No filler** | Every sentence carries weight — cut warmup paragraphs |

## Visual Identity

- **Aesthetic:** Gen Z taste — clean, bold, dark-mode-first, minimal
- **Colors:** Dark backgrounds (#0a0a0a, #111, #1a1a2e), accent: vibrant but controlled
- **Typography:** Modern sans-serif (Inter, Satoshi), clean hierarchy
- **No corporate:** No stock photos, no generic gradients, no blue-and-white SaaS vibes
